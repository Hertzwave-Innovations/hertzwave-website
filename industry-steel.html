Industry

Steel & Metals

Decarbonization in steel hinges on process route shifts (BF-BOF → DRI/EAF), fuel switching (natural gas → green hydrogen), material efficiency (scrap, yield), and electrification. Major challenges include high-capex retrofits, raw-material quality constraints, and real-time factor variability across coke ovens, sinter, and hot metal.

Key drivers & challenges

  • Transition from BF‑BOF to EAF/DRI routes to cut Scope 1 process CO₂.
  • Optimize coke rate, PCI, sinter/PEL and slag chemistry for energy & yield.
  • Increase scrap usage while managing quality; track EAF power intensity.
  • Source low‑carbon electricity; monitor grid factors & on‑site renewables.
  • Meet BRSR/CSRD; align with Worldsteel and GHG Protocol methods.
  • EU CBAM: Carbon border costs on high-emission steel impact export competitiveness; accelerates shift to low-carbon routes (DRI/EAF, green H₂), efficiency, and verified disclosures.
Scope 1
  • BF/BOF off‑gas & combustion (coke ovens, sinter, basic oxygen).
  • DRI reformer fuels; EAF natural gas burners.
  • On‑site mobile & stationary fuel use.
Scope 2
  • Purchased electricity for EAF, casting, rolling.
  • Market vs. location-based factors; REC tracking.
  • Self‑gen/PPAs attribution.
Scope 3 — Upstream
  • Iron ore, pellets, coking coal, limestone factors.
  • Scrap collection/processing emissions.
  • Inbound logistics & contractor fuel.
Scope 3 — Downstream
  • Product transport & distribution.
  • Use‑phase where applicable (e.g., pipelines).
  • End‑of‑life recycling credits.

Compliance & Frameworks

BRSR / SEBI

Automated mapping of emissions, energy, water, and waste indicators with audit trail.

CSRD / ESRS E1–E5

Activity & spend-based support, intensity KPIs (tCO₂e/t crude steel), and scenario-ready notes.

Worldsteel / GHG Protocol

Methodology alignment incl. off‑gas crediting, scrap allocation, and electricity market choices.
