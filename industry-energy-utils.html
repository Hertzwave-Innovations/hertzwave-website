Industry

Energy & Utilities

For electric, gas, and water utilities, decarbonization blends grid modernization, loss reduction, SF₆ & CH₄ abatement, renewable integration & storage, and demand flexibility. Reliability (SAIDI/SAIFI), safety, and affordability are core constraints.

Key drivers & challenges

  • Grid: T&D loss reduction, AMI/MDMS, voltage optimization, DER orchestration.
  • Reliability: SAIDI/SAIFI, outage analytics, vegetation & asset health.
  • Integration: solar/wind, BESS, peak shaving, demand response & TOU tariffs.
  • Fugitives: SF₆ from switchgear, CH₄ from gas networks; LDAR & inventories.
  • Water utilities: NRW reduction, pump efficiency, pressure management.
Scope 1 — Utility operations
  • Fleet fuel, peaker plants start-up fuel (where applicable).
  • Fugitives: SF₆ leaks (switchgear), CH₄ (gas pipelines).
  • Process emissions at water/wastewater treatment.
Scope 2 — Purchased electricity
  • Substations, offices, depots, pumping & treatment plants.
  • Location vs market-based factors, PPAs/RECs.
  • Onsite solar + storage for critical sites.
Scope 3 — Upstream
  • Capital goods: transformers, meters, pipes, pumps.
  • Supplier logistics & contractor activities.
  • Fuel & chemical supply chains for treatment plants.
Scope 3 — Downstream
  • Use of sold products (for gas utilities), customer-side losses (indicative).
  • Franchise/leased assets, distribution losses (optional disclosures).
