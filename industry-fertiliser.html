Industry

Fertilizer

For ammonia, urea & complex fertilizer plants, decarbonization targets feedstock shift (green H₂), process efficiency (SMR/heat recovery), N₂O abatement (nitric acid), CO₂ capture for urea, and electrification of auxiliaries.

Key drivers & challenges

  • Ammonia: NG → green H₂/electrolysis; purge & reformer optimization.
  • SMR/primary reformer: heat recovery, fuel switch, excess air control.
  • Nitric acid: N₂O catalyst (primary/secondary/tertiary) abatement.
  • CO₂ capture & integration with urea synthesis; flare minimization.
  • Utilities: DM water, cooling towers, ZLD; steam/power integration.
  • Circular economy: valorization of industrial byproducts (e.g., steel slag to fertilizer), co-processing, and life-cycle credit accounting.
Scope 1 — Process & Fuel
  • SMR feedstock CO₂, furnace fuel, captive power/gensets.
  • N₂O (nitric acid), CH₄ fugitives, flaring losses.
Scope 2 — Electricity
  • Compressors, pumps, cooling, utilities & offsites.
  • Market vs location-based factors; PPAs/RECs.
Scope 3 — Upstream
  • NG supply chain, catalysts, purchased chemicals & packaging.
  • Capital goods: reactors, compressors, heat exchangers.
Scope 3 — Downstream
  • Distribution (road/rail/port), warehousing, packaging end-of-life.
  • Use-phase N₂O (indicative/optional disclosure).
